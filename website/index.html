QLAB is a digital LAB for the study of cuneiform literacies in Assyria and Babylonia; a playground, as it were, for the development of computational tools to manipulate cuneiform data and obtain new insights in the cuneiform writing systems and their use by scholars and less erudite men and women alike.

The mysteries of cuneiform writing

Past, present and future of this project

QLAB is born out of a previous, small scale research project into the orthography of Neo-Assyrian letter writers as known from the publication of their correspondence in the State Archives of Assyria, volumes 1, 5, 10, 13, 15, 16, and 19, in addition to the Neo-Assyrian letters from the reign of Assurbanipal as present in the Helsinki database.

Recently, the State Archives of Assyria have become easily and freely accessible online as part of the Open Richly-Annotated Cuneiform Corpus (Oracc), together with other cuneiform text corpora such as the Corpus of Ancient Mesopotamian Scholarship and the Royal Inscriptions of the Neo-Assyrian Period.
